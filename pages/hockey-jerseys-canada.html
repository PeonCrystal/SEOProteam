Three Ways to Build Your Custom Hockey Jerseys

Not every team needs the same type of jersey. We offer three customization methods — each with its own look, feel, and price point. Not sure which is right for your team? Read our sublimated vs stitched comparison guide for the full breakdown.

Sublimated Hockey Jerseys

Sublimated hockey jerseys use a dye process that bonds colour permanently into the fabric fibre. The result is unlimited colour options, complex artwork, gradients, and photo-quality graphics with zero cracking, peeling, or fading.

  • Unlimited colours at no extra cost
  • Lightweight, breathable polyester mesh
  • Colours won't crack, peel, or wash out
  • Best for: beer league teams, recreational leagues, tournaments, creative designs

Stitched Hockey Jerseys

Stitched hockey jerseys deliver the premium, textured look you see in professional leagues. Names, numbers, and logos are individually cut and sewn onto the jersey for a raised, tactile finish with a heavier, traditional weight.

  • Professional raised lettering and numbering
  • Heavier, traditional weight and feel
  • Premium pro-level look and texture
  • Best for: competitive rep hockey, organizations wanting a professional finish

Tackle Twill Hockey Jerseys

Tackle twill hockey jerseys bridge the gap between sublimated and fully stitched. Twill letters and numbers are heat-applied and stitched down for a polished, durable look that holds up through seasons of physical play.

  • Clean, professional appearance
  • Durable heat-applied and stitched construction
  • Middle ground between sublimated and fully stitched
  • Best for: minor hockey associations, house leagues, teams wanting a clean pro look
