Materials and Construction

Sublimated Jersey Materials

Sublimated hockey jerseys are made from pro-weight polyester mesh that is breathable, moisture-wicking, and built for game play. The sublimation process infuses dye directly into the fabric fibre at a molecular level — meaning colours become a permanent part of the jersey. There is no surface layer that can crack, peel, or wash away, even after hundreds of machine washes.

Stitched Jersey Materials

Stitched hockey jerseys use a heavier polyester base with individually cut twill or felt pieces for names, numbers, and logos. Each element is sewn directly onto the jersey with reinforced stitching — the same construction method used in professional leagues. The result is a raised, tactile finish that looks and feels premium.

Tackle Twill Materials

Tackle twill hockey jerseys feature heat-applied polyester twill that is then stitched down for additional durability. This gives you the clean, layered look of stitched construction with a flatter profile and slightly lower price point.
