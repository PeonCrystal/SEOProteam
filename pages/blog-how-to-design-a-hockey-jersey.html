How to Design a Hockey Jersey: The Complete Guide

Designing a custom hockey jersey for your team is about more than picking colours and slapping on a logo. A well-designed jersey builds team identity, looks sharp on the ice, and holds up visually for years. Whether you're starting from scratch or refreshing an existing look, here are the key principles that separate great hockey jersey designs from forgettable ones.

Why Jersey Design Matters

Your jersey is the most visible piece of your team's identity. It's the first thing opponents see on the ice, the first thing fans notice in the stands, and the thing your players pull on before every game. A strong hockey jersey design creates pride, builds camaraderie, and gives your team a professional presence — no matter what level you play at.

Tip 1: Start with a Colour Scheme That Works

Colour is the foundation of every jersey design. Choose your colours deliberately, not randomly.

Colour Psychology on the Ice

  • Dark colours (black, navy, dark green) — project power, intimidation, and seriousness
  • Bright colours (red, orange, yellow) — high energy, visibility, and aggression
  • Cool colours (blue, teal, grey) — calm, confidence, and professionalism
  • White / light colours — clean, classic, and traditional

Practical Colour Tips

  • Limit your palette — 2–3 main colours plus an accent colour is ideal. Too many colours create visual noise.
  • Ensure contrast — names and numbers must be readable from across the rink. If your jersey is dark, your lettering should be light (and vice versa).
  • Consider home and away — if you're ordering two sets, design them as a pair. A dark home jersey and a light away jersey with the same design language creates a cohesive look.
  • Check your league's rules — some leagues have colour requirements or restrictions on home vs away.

Tip 2: Get Your Logo Placement and Sizing Right

Your team logo is the centrepiece of the jersey. It needs to command attention without overwhelming the design.

  • Centre chest — the traditional placement. Size it so it's visible and balanced but doesn't extend into the arm or shoulder area.
  • Shoulder patches — great for secondary logos, league crests, or alternate branding. Keep them sized proportionally.
  • Don't stretch or distort — if your logo is wide, don't force it into a tall space. Work with the natural proportions.
  • Use vector files — AI, EPS, or SVG formats produce the sharpest print. If you only have a JPG or PNG, our design team can recreate it.

Tip 3: Choose Fonts That Are Readable, Not Just Cool

The font you pick for names and numbers affects both aesthetics and function. It needs to be readable from across the ice — not just up close.

  • Block fonts — clean, traditional, and always readable. The safest choice for most teams.
  • Script fonts — stylish and distinctive, but test readability at a distance. Works better for team names on the front than player names on the back.
  • Custom / display fonts — can look great but watch for readability issues with smaller text like player names.
  • Number style — match your number font to your name font for a cohesive look. Avoid overly decorative numbers that are hard to read quickly.

Tip 4: Avoid Common Design Mistakes

We've seen thousands of jersey designs. Here are the mistakes that come up most often:

  • Too many colours — more than 3–4 colours usually hurts the design. Less is more.
  • Low contrast names and numbers — if the ref can't read your number, it's a problem. Always prioritize readability.
  • Overcrowded designs — not every square inch needs artwork. Negative space is your friend.
  • Ignoring the back — the back of the jersey is what people see most during a game. Make sure names and numbers look clean from behind.
  • Following trends too closely — trendy designs can look dated within a year or two. Classic elements age better.

Tip 5: Test Your Design Before Committing

A design that looks great on a screen might look different on an actual jersey. Here's how to validate before you commit:

  • Get a professional proof — at Pro Team Jerseys, every order includes a free digital mockup before production
  • Share with the team — get input from players. If multiple people flag the same issue, listen.
  • View at actual size — zoom out or print the proof at scale to see how it reads from a distance
  • Check both dark and light versions — if you're ordering home and away, review both as a pair

Tip 6: Think About Durability When You Design

Your hockey jersey design needs to hold up through a full season of play and washing. Some design choices are more durable than others:

  • Sublimated jerseys — any design you can imagine will last because the dye is bonded into the fabric. Go as complex as you want.
  • Stitched jerseys — keep lettering and numbering sizes standard. Very small stitched elements can be harder to maintain over time.
  • White areas — white elements on sublimated jerseys may show staining more than darker colours. Consider this for the lower portions of the jersey.

Tip 7: Think About the Long Game

A great jersey design should last your team for years. Consider these long-term factors:

  • Reorders — will you need to add jerseys for new players? Sublimated jerseys guarantee exact colour matching across orders. Stitched jerseys also match well across batches.
  • Sponsor changes — if sponsors rotate, consider placing logos in areas that are easy to update or swap.
  • Timelessness — a jersey you love today should still look good in 3–5 years. Lean toward clean design over fleeting trends.
